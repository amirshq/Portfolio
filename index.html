PartPricingTool

PartPricingTool is an end-to-end AI-driven pricing and categorization platform designed for engineering and estimation teams. It ingests large, heterogeneous part catalogs from relational databases, applies data cleaning and feature enrichment, and leverages large language models and rule-based logic to automatically classify parts, standardize nomenclature, and infer pricing attributes. Built on a scalable backend (FastAPI, SQL Server) with a modern React frontend, the system reduces manual effort, improves pricing consistency, and enables faster, data-driven decision-making in complex estimation workflows.

Extracto - LLM and RAG Chatbot

Extracto is a retrieval-augmented generation (RAG)–based technical assistant built to query and reason over large collections of domain-specific engineering documents. The platform performs document ingestion, OCR, intelligent chunking, embedding, and vector search to retrieve high-relevance context, which is then combined with LLMs to generate accurate, grounded answers to technical questions. Designed for production use, Extracto improves knowledge accessibility, reduces search time across PDFs and manuals, and supports engineers with reliable, explainable responses derived directly from authoritative source documents.

Churn Prediction

The churn prediction initiative focuses on the early identification of at-risk customers to facilitate proactive interventions. By prioritizing high-risk accounts, this strategy aims to protect revenue streams and effectively reduce churn through targeted account management.

ML Models for Stockholm Energy Forecasting

Over the course of this project, I tuned ML and artificial neural network (ANN) models using extensive datasets obtained from KTH and the City of Stockholm, focusing on building energy performance and district heating demand patterns. Leveraging clustering methodologies such as K-means and hierarchical techniques, I identified coherent thermal demand segments and extracted features critical for downstream forecasting. These structured insights were used to develop advanced machine learning models and pipelines that could predict energy consumption trends and future heating/cooling load with improved precision, supporting sustainable urban energy planning. To ensure practical usability of these models by research stakeholders, I built Flask-based backend services that exposed the predictive analytics through robust APIs, enabling seamless integration with existing research tools and dashboards. The end-to-end solution encompassed data ingestion, model training, validation, and deployment, providing actionable forecasts and visualizations that informed municipal decision-making on energy monitoring and infrastructure placement. The implementation demonstrated how data-driven modeling and API-centric delivery can operationalize complex ML workflows in real-world smart city environments.
